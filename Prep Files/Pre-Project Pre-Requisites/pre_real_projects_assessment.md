## Weaknesses

**1. Dependency on Guided Instructions (Critical)**  
Across all four mocks, Claude decided what to build, which fields to use, what formula to write, where to drag things. When guidance stopped, progress stopped.

**2. SQL Writing from Scratch (Major)**  
Can follow SQL logic when explained. Understands what CTEs and window functions do conceptually. But sitting in front of a schema with a business question and writing the query independently — that bridge hasn't been crossed.

**3. Tool Mechanics Not in Muscle Memory (Moderate)**  
Significant time spent figuring out where things are in the interface (e.g., "Compute Using" struggle in Tableau) rather than focusing on analytical decisions.

**4. Visual Storytelling is Reactive, Not Proactive (Moderate)**  
Discovers a visual doesn't fit after building it, rather than analyzing the data shape first and choosing the right visual upfront.

---

## Root Cause: Passive Execution vs Active Ownership

All four weaknesses share one root cause.

During mock projects, instructions went from screen to hands — skipping the brain. Claude said "build a bar chart with revenue by region" and it got built. Claude said "write this CTE" and it got written. The *what* and *why* behind each decision was never personally wrestled with.

**Evidence:** 120+ SQL problems solved and 4 mocks completed — yet still at 23/40. Volume of practice wasn't the issue. The *mode* of practice was.

**Counter-evidence:** The moments of genuine brilliance (rejecting scatter plot, designing per-segment analysis, hybrid Python strategy, Sentinel differentiation) all share one trait — Claude didn't instruct those decisions. They came from actively questioning, challenging, and deciding independently.

**The fix:** Not more practice. Better practice. Every decision must pass through the brain before reaching the hands. Ask "why this table, why this column, why this visual, why this layout" BEFORE executing.

## Real Projects: Guidance vs Ownership Contract

### What Claude Provides:
- Project domain and business story
- Database schema design (table structure, relationships)
- List of business questions the dashboard should answer
- Architecture decisions (Medallion layers, API choices)
- 12 differentiator checklist per project
- Review and feedback AFTER you build something

### What I Own:
- Looking at the schema and deciding which tables and columns to use
- Writing every SQL query from scratch
- Choosing which visualization fits each business question
- Designing the dashboard layout
- Debugging when something breaks
- Documenting my work

### When Stuck:
Claude does NOT give the answer. Claude asks questions until I find it myself. That is the Socratic contract.

### The Test:
Can I walk into an interview and defend every query, every dashboard, every architectural decision as MY OWN WORK? If no — I was in passive mode. If yes — I owned it.
